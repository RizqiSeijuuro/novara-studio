Case Study · Concept 01

Meter — making usage-based billing feel trustworthy.

A landing page concept for a usage analytics platform that sells to two skeptical audiences at once: engineers who hate marketing, and finance teams who hate surprises.

Type — Concept project Scope — Strategy, copy, design, build Timeline — 7 days
Open the live concept ↗
The brief

Billing tools all claim "clarity." Almost none of them look like they mean it.

Meter turns raw product usage into billable events and forecastable revenue. The category problem: every competitor page is a wall of abstract gradients and vague promises — exactly the aesthetic that makes engineers close the tab. The page had to prove precision before saying a word about it.

The approach

Show the product before the pitch. A working dashboard mock — animated bars, tabular numbers, a live-status pill — sits directly under the headline. If the product is about seeing your usage clearly, the hero should demonstrate exactly that.

Treat code as content. The integration section is a real code block, not a screenshot of one. Engineers evaluate tools by reading the API, so the API is on the page.

Engineering-paper aesthetic. A faint 48px grid on the background, hairline borders, and mono labels give the whole page the texture of a spec sheet — trust through discipline rather than decoration.

Design decisions

Typography

Space Grotesk headlines for a technical-but-warm voice, Inter Tight body, and IBM Plex Mono doing real work: labels, metrics, code, axes.

Color

Paper and ink, with green reserved for money and live data — and one amber warning bar in the chart, because honest dashboards have warnings.

Layout

Centered hero into a strict card grid. Everything sits on the background's 48px grid, so the page reads as engineered, not arranged.

Motion

One meaningful animation: chart bars scale up when the dashboard scrolls into view. Data arriving — not decoration moving.
